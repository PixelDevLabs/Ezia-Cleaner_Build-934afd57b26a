The First Time

The first time you go Postal, you might want to adjust the difficulty level. Do this by selecting OPTIONS then DIFFICULTY. If you can't figure out how to use the menus, see menus. Set the difficulty as low as you can without feeling like a complete wimp. Then press 'Esc' a few times to return to the main menu.

Now select START and then SINGLE PLAYER and then NEW GAME.  As you might have guessed, this will start a new, single-player game, beginning on level 1.

You control the Postal Dude using the keyboard, mouse, or a combination of the two. The default control settings are similar to many other shooter-style games. You basically turn or rotate the Postal Dude to face a particular direction and then walk/run in that direction. See controls for details.

When you first start playing, you'll want to be shooting almost constantly, so hold down the left mouse button or the 'Ctrl' key. To help you get used to aiming, you might want to press 'F2' to turn on the crosshairs.

Your goal is to complete all the levels without being killed and ultimately to reach the end of the game.

Mission Goal

To complete a level, you must kill a certain percentage of the "hostiles" (the characters that are trying to kill you). This "mission goal" is displayed in the upper status bar when the level starts. You can redisplay your mission goal at any time by pressing F5.

The number of hostiles and the total population are displayed in the upper status bar. Once you have met your mission goal, you can press F1 to go on to the next level. Or you can stick around and do more damage before you decide to move on.

Life

The Postal Dude only has a single life. He can pick up health packs along the way, and he also gets a health boost between levels. Body armor is very useful, and can often be found laying around. Or maybe hidden behind something so it isn't too obvious.

If the Postal Dude dies before reaching the end of a level, that's it -- the level is over. You can press 'space' to restart that level.

Saving

If you want to save your current game, press ESC to bring up the in-game menu. This will pause your game while you save (choose the SAVE item from the menu). Note that when you go back to a saved game, you start over at the beginning of the level you saved on and with whatever weapons you had at the start of that level. Yes, it's harder this way, but it prevents the kidn of "cheating" that's possible when you can save your game every few seconds and pick up exactly where you left off. After you're done saving, you can continue the game from where it was paused.

Other Stuff

If you just need to pause your game, just press the 'Pause' key.

If you want to give up, press Q and the Postal Dude will commit suicide.
