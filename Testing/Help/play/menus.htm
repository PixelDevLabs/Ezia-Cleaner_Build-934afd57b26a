Menus

In the game, a series of menu screens allows you to make choices and change options.

  • Press the 'up arrow' and 'down arrow' cursor keys to move the bullet up and down.

  • Press 'Enter' when the bullet is next to the item you want to choose.

  • Some menu items link to additional menus. Press 'ESC' to go back to the previous menu.

Each menu is described below, starting with the first menu that appears in the game.

You can click on various menu items below and they will take you to the associated menu, just as if you had chosen that item in the game.

SINGLE PLAYER MENU
SPECIAL DELIVERY LEVELS Start a game using only the Special Delivery levels.
NEW GAME (ALL LEVELS) Start a new, single-player game beginning on level 1 and proceeding through all the original and Special Delivery levels combined.

CHALLENGE MENU

The challenge games have a variety of scoring methods and goals.

The goal is displayed below your score at the start of a level.  Press F5 to see the goal again.

A separate high-score table is maintained for each challenge catagory.
THE GAUNTLET Play the entire series of challenge levels, one after another, from start to finish.
TIMED Let's you select any of the timed levels.  These levels have a timer that counts down to 0, and you must accomplish the stated goal before time runs out.
GOAL Let's you select any of the goal-oriented levels.  These levels have a clock that times how long it takes you to complete the stated goal.
FLAG Let's you select any of the capture-the-flag levels.  These levels have a clock that times how long it takes you to capture the flag.
CHECKPOINT Let's you select any of the checkpoint levels.  These levels are similar to racing-style games, where you have a set amount of time to complete the course.  You need to pick up the flags (checkpoints) along the way, some of which will extend your time.  The goal is to pick up as many flags as possible before time finally runs out.

KEYBOARD/MOUSE/JOYSTICK SETUP MENU

These three menu screens allow you to map a key, a mouse button, or a joystick button, respectively, onto any of the listed actions. They also allow you to restore the original control settings.

To map a key or button onto a particular item, move the bullet to that item and press 'Enter'. Then press whatever key or mouse button or joystick button you want to map onto that item. The key or button name will appear in the box next to the item.

For mouse buttons, you can use button combinations! For instance, you can map the left button to one item, the right button to another, and the two buttons together to a third item! This allows for much greater flexibility.

There are two items each for Fire, Run and Strafe, which lets you map two different keys/buttons onto each of these actions. This allows for some advanced capabilities.

Example #1: You normally press 'Ctrl' to fire your weapon. But when you try to fire while strafing, you find that it's difficult to press both 'Ctrl' (to fire) and 'Alt' (to strafe). What you really want is for the character to automatically fire while strifing. That's easy. Just map 'Alt' to the second "Fire" item ('Ctrl' is already mapped to the first). Now, whenever you press 'Alt' to strafe, the character also fires!

Example #2: Many players want the 'left-arrow' and 'right-arrow' keys to make the character strafe left and right. Simply map 'left-arrow' to both "Left" and the first "Strafe", and then map 'right-arrow' to "Right" and the second "Strafe". Mission accomplished.

To restore all the keys or buttons to their original default values, move the bullet to RESTORE DEFAULTS and press 'Enter'.

PERFORMANCE MENU

All of these options are enabled by default.  If you have a slower machine, you can trade off some cool features for faster frame rates.  They are listed in order, starting with the ones that use the most CPU power.

For each item, press 'Enter' to turn the check mark on or off.
TRANSPARENCY Transparency is what makes it possible to see other objects through smoke, fire or fog. Disabling this turns smoke, fire and fog and other similar effects into solid objects that you can't see through at all.
3D LIGHTING This gives the 3D objects a rounder, softer appearance. Disabling this makes them look worse.
PARTICLES Particle effects are used to show shotgun blasts, blood splatters, and bullet casings. Disabling this turns those things off.
DYNAMIC VOLUME Dynamic volume makes things louder and softer as you get closer and further away from things. If you turn this off, all the sounds will always be the same volume, making it difficult to tell how close things are.
AMBIENT SOUNDS These are sounds that add to the overall atmosphere of a particular level, but are not critical to the gameplay.
back to previous menu

 

DIFFICULTY MENU

The slider bar on this menu ranges from 1 to 11 and has four color zones.

The numbers represent how accurate the enemy characters are when they shoot, how often they shoot, and how much damage you take.

The color zones correspond to different level layouts: easy, medium, hard and nightmare.  The easy levels have more hit points, more ammo, more powerups, fewer enemies, and less powerful weapons being used against you.  As you move up the scale, things get more difficult, with more enemies using more powerful weapons and less ammo and powerups to help you.
