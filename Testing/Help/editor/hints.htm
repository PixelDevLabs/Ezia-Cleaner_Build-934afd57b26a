Helpful Hints

The following hints are from Cordell Falk of Deathblade.

1) Since it is not currently possible to easily edit graphics, the key to making a good level will be to change the style of gameplay. The original Postal levels were all a variety of 'hunt them all down' missions where you start near the outer perimeter and shoot your way around and through. With the Deathblade levels - I changed the perspective around. Some missions you start in the middle, facing attackers incoming from all sides. One mission made use of a flag search. Throwing the player 'right into the thick of it' really changes the feeling.

2) Most of the Deathblade missions used a specific GOAL setting to keep things interesting. One way to make people keep playing the mission is if they have a score to try and beat - time, kills, etc. People can then challenge themselves, or their friends, and get more value from the add-on. Also, by setting a GOAL of # OF KILLS, you can force the player to wipe out the civilians to reach the winning total. With the GOAL set to a kill percentage, it only includes HOSTILES.

3) DISPENSERS - Dispensers are really fun to work with. The machine gun allows the player to shoot enemies that are not even in view range yet - thus keeping damage (and unfortunately action) to a minimum. Numerous dispensers keeps the player on their toes - because now they must watch for existing enemies, and additional people that will appear out of nowhere. Mixing up range and timed dispensers is entertaining.

4) MORE WITH DISPENSERS - Rather than set a time limit, which abruptly ends the mission and isn't realistic, you can set dispensers on the map with delay times equal to the time limit you would like for a level. Therefore, if you think a player should kill the 80 people on your level in 5 minutes, you can set the kills GOAL to 80 and then set up a dispenser to crank out, say, 30 swat men with assault rifles at the 5 minute mark. Think of it as 'reinforcements' - much more fun than being interrupted by a 'high score list'.

5) Players expect enemies to be waiting at ground level, so why not throw a few of them up on a rooftop? Flamer-carrying swat men dropping on your head is... interesting. Of course, you CANNOT put them on GUARD logic or else they will sit on the roof and you usually won't be able to hit them. Default logic will make them fire at you, and drop down to ground level when you run away.

6) MAPS - You can't make your own maps, but there are different possibilities! You can load up any of the original maps, or load up the multiplayer versions, which are smaller. Also, some of the Gauntlet missions are ones you don't see in the original campaign levels. Play around with it!

7) TRICKS - Here are some various tricks to make a level more difficult and give the player more to look for...

- hide powerups behind barrels, mask them behind trees, etc.

- Use mines! If something is really important, the enemies could have placed some mines around for protection. Of course, the computer runs into them a lot as well, so be careful with placement.

- Place weapons strategically around the map - don't just start the player with all the weapons, make them search around for them! Better yet, make a section of the map 'unbeatable' unless you clear out a different section and get the power-ups there first.

8) SENTRIES - These are fun. Who says a sentry has to fire missiles? Stick one behind a building or tree and let it shoot flames, or napalm. Be strategic with their placement - set a few nearby and modify their firing ranges so that all of them fire simultaneously when you walk into an area. Or if they shoot missiles - perhaps speed up their firing rates so that you REALLY have to be careful. Play around - there are a lot of different combinations for these, so experiment.

9) TRAPS - Aside from hiding sentries and people behind walls and trees, you can also set traps with barrels. Place barrels in an area you are sure the player will use and have enemies placed to blow them up. Or place a mine in between some barrels so the player will never see it coming. Be creative.

10) MAKING THE PEOPLE FIGHT BACK - This one is pretty funny. Place civilians on the map - The Man, Woman, Man Frank, or Woman Mindy. If you leave them with default logic, you can make them 'attack'. Give them either grenades, dynamite, or a cocktail. When you kill these characters, there is about a 50% chance that they will 'fire' their weapon at you. This is used in level 10 of the Deathblade campaign. By place a large number of civilians with the 'potential' for throwing explosives, the level becomes much more interesting. Every time you kill one, you've gotta prepare to run.

11) LOGICS - Keeping it simple is best. The pylons can create some neat tricks, but they are not really necessary to build a fast-paced level. The three easiest to work with are guard, fodder, and lurkmed.Guard caused the person to stand around until you come into range. This can create problems, because players can just shoot this enemy from across the screen and score kills without a real fight. Fodder makes them rush you and is supposed to make them fire like mad. Many times though they do a lot of running, but not a lot of shooting. Good luck with that one. Lurkmed is a great logic because the enemy will generally stay where you put them, but as you get near, the enemy will put up a good fight. They dodge bullets, and position themselves for better shots. This usually keeps the enemy from being a free kill.

12) REMEMBER COMPUTER SPEED! - Postal is a pretty heavy duty game and it takes some considerable processor power to run. If you make a million dispensers or exploding barrels, the level is going to run slow and players are going to get annoyed. Keep excess to a minimum.
